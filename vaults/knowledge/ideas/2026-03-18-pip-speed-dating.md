## The spark
People avoid governance because it feels like homework. Make it social and time-boxed.

## The idea
Run a monthly **PIP Speed-Dating** voice event:
- 5–7 proposal ideas (or open questions) get **3 minutes each**: premise + what decision is needed.
- Then 2-minute rapid Q&A.
- End with a community poll: “Which two should become a real draft?”

Important: it’s *not* a vote replacement—just a literacy + ideation format.

## Why it could work
- Time pressure prevents rambling and keeps newcomers engaged.
- Produces a clear shortlist and volunteer list.
- Normalizes governance as a community hangout.

## Why it might not
- Can attract debates without conclusions if not facilitated.
- Some topics may need sensitivity/moderation.

## What it would take
- A facilitator + timer.
- A simple submission form for proposal pitches.
- A follow-up doc summarizing the shortlist and next steps.

## Adjacent ideas
- “Governance office hours” paired with the event.
- A ‘PIP buddy’ system (pair a newcomer with a veteran for one cycle).
