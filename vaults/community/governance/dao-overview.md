# Star Atlas DAO — Governance Overview

The Star Atlas DAO is the decentralized governance body that oversees the political, economic, and developmental direction of the Star Atlas metaverse. It is accessible at [govern.staratlas.com](https://govern.staratlas.com/).

## What the DAO Governs

POLIS holders have authority over key aspects of the Star Atlas ecosystem:

- **Treasury allocations** — how DAO funds are deployed
- **Game features** — prioritization and implementation of gameplay changes
- **Economic parameters** — marketplace fees, reward distribution rates, sink mechanisms
- **Ecosystem development** — partnerships, grants, community initiatives
- **Protocol upgrades** — changes to on-chain programs and governance itself

## POLIS Voting Power (PVP)

To participate in governance, players must become POLIS Voting Power (PVP) holders by locking [[token-economics|POLIS tokens]] in the POLIS Locker protocol.

**How voting power is calculated:**
- Voting power = POLIS locked × remaining lock duration (in years)
- Example: 1,000 POLIS locked for 4 years = 4,000 PVP
- As lock duration decays, voting power decreases proportionally
- Longer commitments yield more influence

This time-weighted design serves multiple purposes: it incentivizes long-term alignment with the ecosystem, prevents flash-loan governance attacks, and ensures that those with the most influence have the most to lose from poor decisions.

## POLIS Staking Rewards

Locked POLIS accrues additional POLIS rewards over time, creating an incentive to participate in governance beyond voting rights alone.

## Governance Portal

The governance portal at [govern.staratlas.com](https://govern.staratlas.com/) is where PVP holders:

- View active and historical proposals
- Participate in deliberation and debate
- Cast votes on [[pip-lifecycle|PIPs (Polity Improvement Proposals)]]
- Monitor treasury and governance metrics

## Governance Principles

The Star Atlas DAO aims to balance decentralization with effective decision-making:

- Proposals follow a structured [[pip-lifecycle|PIP lifecycle]] with deliberation periods
- Non-binding Public Interest Voting gauges sentiment before formal votes
- Live debate sessions provide space for community discussion
- The DAO is designed to evolve — governance parameters themselves can be changed through governance
