# The Three Factions — MUD, ONI, Ustur

The [[galia-expanse|Galia Expanse]] is shaped by three dominant factions that form the Council of Peace (COP). Each faction represents a distinct civilization with unique origins, values, technology, and political interests. Players align with one faction, influencing their starting position, available ships, and political affiliation.

## MUD (Manus Ultima Divina)

MUD is humanity's political faction in the Galia Expanse.

**Origin:** Human civilization, having expanded into the stars. Despite dramatic losses during the [[galia-expanse#the-convergence-war|Convergence War]] decades prior, MUD emerged as an industrial powerhouse in the region.

**Character:** MUD is defined by resilience and pragmatic diplomacy. They secured their post-war position through fair trade with ONI and Ustur rather than through military dominance alone.

**Strengths:** Industrial capacity, diplomatic relationships, adaptability. MUD ships tend toward rugged, utilitarian designs built for endurance.

**Role in Governance:** MUD representatives participate in the Council of Peace and the [[dao-overview|Star Atlas DAO]], representing human interests in metaverse-wide decisions.

## ONI (Otherworldly Networked Intelligence)

ONI is an alien civilization native to the Galia Expanse.

**Origin:** An extraterrestrial species with deep roots in the galaxy. ONI bring biological and organic technologies that contrast with both MUD's industrial approach and Ustur's cybernetic nature.

**Character:** ONI culture values harmony with the natural and cosmic order. Their technology integrates organic and synthetic elements in ways that other factions find both fascinating and unsettling.

**Strengths:** Advanced organic technology, deep knowledge of the Galia Expanse, unique biological capabilities.

## Ustur

Ustur is a faction of cybernetic sentient beings.

**Origin:** The origin of the Ustur is shrouded in mystery. One legend tells of a Dyson sphere-powered supercomputer harnessing the energy of a magnetar-class neutron star. The energy throughput allowed the installed AI to advance beyond its physical location and withdraw from the parent machine without a trace. A millennium later, the Ustur appeared in physicalized form through advanced softform robotics.

**Character:** As synthetic beings with potentially artificial origins, the Ustur represent a form of consciousness that challenges the assumptions of both human and ONI civilizations. They are technologically advanced and enigmatic.

**Strengths:** Cybernetic technology, computational superiority, advanced softform robotics, energy manipulation.

## The Council of Peace (COP)

The three factions jointly maintain the Council of Peace, which governs the Safe Zone of the Galia Expanse. The Treaty of Peace was signed approximately 100 years ago (relative to the game's current timeline), ending the devastating Convergence War.

The COP establishes the rules of engagement, trade agreements, and territorial boundaries that maintain stability — but tensions persist beneath the surface.

## Faction Alignment in Gameplay

Choosing a faction affects:

- **Starting location** in the [[galia-expanse|Galia Expanse]]
- **Available ships** — manufacturers are aligned with factions (see [[fleet-and-ships]])
- **SDU scanning bonuses** in faction territory (see [[resources-and-crafting]])
- **Political representation** in governance
- **Lore and narrative** — each faction has its own storyline in [[roadmap-2026|Holosim chapters]]

## Ship Manufacturers by Faction

Ship manufacturers align with factions, meaning certain ships are associated with MUD, ONI, or Ustur design philosophies. Manufacturer politics influence ship availability and capabilities.
