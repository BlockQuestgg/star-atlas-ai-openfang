# Fleet & Ship Guide

Ships are the primary assets in Star Atlas. They are NFTs on the Solana blockchain, tradeable on the [[galactic-marketplace|Galactic Marketplace]], and stakeable into [[sage-overview|SAGE Labs]] for economic gameplay.

## Ship Class Sizes

Ships are classified by size, which determines their approximate dimensions, power level, crew capacity, and resource consumption:

| Class | Scale | Typical Role |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| XX-Small | Smallest | Solo fighters, scouts |
| X-Small | Very small | Light fighters, interceptors |
| Small | Small | Multi-role fighters, light freighters, bounty hunters |
| Medium | Medium | Core fleet workhorses, freighters, support ships |
| Large | Large | Heavy combat, large freighters, fleet support |
| Capital | Very large | Fleet flagships, mobile bases |
| Commander | Massive | Strategic command vessels |
| Titan | Largest | Superstructures, endgame assets |

## Ship Specifications (Specs)

Each ship has a primary specification that defines its combat and economic role:

- **Fighter** — Combat-focused, available from XX-Small to Titan
- **Bomber** — Area damage, available from XX-Small to Titan
- **Bounty Hunter** — Pursuit and interdiction, Small to Large
- **Freighter** — Cargo hauling and trade, Small to Commander
- **Refuel/Repair** — Fleet support and logistics, Small to Capital

The Ship Configuration V3 system expanded these roles, allowing deeper specialization and loadout customization.

## Fleet Formation in SAGE

To use ships in SAGE Labs, players stake them into the Fleet Formation program. Key mechanics:

- Ships must be staked to participate — they cannot be traded or moved while staked
- Fleets can be composed of multiple ships for combined capabilities
- Fleet composition affects mining efficiency, cargo capacity, scanning range, and combat effectiveness
- Each ship consumes R4 resources (Fuel, Food, Ammo, Toolkits) at rates determined by its class and spec

## Ship Manufacturers

Ships are produced by various in-lore manufacturers, each aligned with one of the three factions ([[factions|MUD, ONI, or Ustur]]). Manufacturer politics influence ship availability and faction bonuses. See [[factions]] for faction details.

## Ship Rarity

Ships come in different rarity tiers affecting their stats and value: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary, and Anomaly.

## Acquiring Ships

Ships can be acquired through:

- The [[galactic-marketplace|Galactic Marketplace]] (primary and secondary sales)
- Galactic Asset Offerings (GAOs) — periodic NFT drops
- In-game crafting (future, with C4 update)
- Secondary markets on Solana (e.g., Tensor, Magic Eden)
